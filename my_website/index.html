Trappist Beers!

Trappist monsteries are known for having wonderful, delicious beers. The monks have a rich culture of beer making sine the middle ages. The first official Trappist brewery order began in France in 1685, there are currently 13 official breweries in the world. To be an official Trappist Brewery it be authicated and is allowed to use the Authentic Trappist Product label.

How do you know if it's a true Trappist beer?

To be considered a true Trappist beer these critera have to be met:

  1. It can only be brewed with in a Trappist monastery, by monks or nuns, or supervised by them.
  2. The brewery cannot be the main objective of the monastery.
  3. The brewery is not intended to be a profit making business, and any additional funds over the cost of living for the monks and monastery must be donated to people in need.

Types of Trappist beer

The most popular tyes of beer that are brewed are called Single (blonde), Dubbel, Tripple or Quadruple which describes how much malt is in each style and each is progressively richer in color appearance. Enkels were originally meant for the monks themselves, as it has the lowest alchol by volume (ABV%). There is only one Trappist monasetry in the United States, and it is located in Massachusetts.
