How I became involved in web development

I guess you could say I became involved in web devolopment by accident. It was mid November 2018. You could smell all the fixings just a few weeks away. When I struck up a conversation with my cousin Tyler. Come to find out that he had just finished a program for computer coding called Turing.

"Ya thats cool, I guess" I thought to myself. The more Tyler talked about it the more exciting he became and the more excited I became! He said I had to look it up, but He couldn't even wait to for me to get home to check it out. So he whipped his phone out of his pocket and wham! I was hooked instantly. The next day I was practicing code.

My journey was just beginning

After that first initial explotion of excitment that followed that night. I hit the ground running and I wasnt going to stop! After the holidays had passed self motivation and Tyler for a resource I was making leaps and bounds! Or so it felt...

My cousin was off, living the dream as the say. And I, well I had to start my journey. So I began to create my own CodePen resume-page to show as a part of my application to Turing. As research went I Knew I needed to get an understanding on a few things like HTML, CSS and Javascript

Once I had familiarized myself with those three key components. I had come across a few different websites that were a must know on my way to apply all this new info and skills. These websites were:Turing,GitHub,Slack.

Getting started is staying motivated

After spending almost a 6 months dabbling around the application process and the inevitably of choosing a start date. I found myself becoming complacent. The at home self tought lessons were becoming boring and farther and fewer in between. To be honest I was off the wagon completely.

To rub the salt in even more! My other cousin Alyssa had not only started Turing and was kicking ass, but was well in to MOD3. I knew I had to do something about this rut I created.

So I made a phone call to Alyssa. After what seemed like the best phone call of my life. I knew what I needed to do. That was to just make the jump, to stay motivated. That night I sat down and applied. Later that week I was accepted and my trains engine was roaring once again!

There is a better life that I want to live, and now that I have a start date. I am motivated by the challenge and the responsibility to changing my life for the better and getting out of my own way.
