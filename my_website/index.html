Exploring My Passions in Mental Health

Collin Kallery

Ever since I was young, I had always been very interested in helping others, working with kids, and understanding the mind. I have always felt that compassion is one of my greatest strengths, along with one of the greatest strengths of humankind as a whole. Compassion brings us all onto the same level - it allows us to remove ourselves from our own individual experience for a few brief moments in an attempt to further understand someone's current state. Compassion is one of the most important ingredients in therapeutic settings as well - when a client is struggling and feels as if the entire world is crashing down on him or her, having a trained professional connect with him or her and validate their feelings is one of the first major steps in helping that person. Compassion unites us, and throughout my years I have worked hard to dedicate my life to it. As I move forward into the world of coding and technology, I hope to employ compassionate, therapeutic values into my daily work.

Prior Experience

  • Gymnastics Coach, City of Boulder: For about two years I worked as a gymnastics coach for the City of Boulder, teaching for ages between 2.5 and 13. Eventually,I became head gymnastics coach, and later I specialized in working with children with various developmental disabilities during our gymnastics classes on a one-on-one basis.
  • Behavioral Technician, Aspire Autism: I had a year-long internship with a company called Aspire Autism in Broomfield, where I learned how to employ a therapeutic treatment called ABA, or Applied Behavioral Analysis, for children with Autism.
  • Research Assistant, Cognitive Development Center CU Boulder: For a year and a half, I worked as an assistant in a research lab at the University of Colorado, where we studied mindfulness in children between the ages of 3 and 11.
  • Research Assitant, CREST Lab: I spent about a year working at another lab at CU Boulder where we studied postpartum depression in women.
  • Teaching Assistant, Research Methods in Clinical Psychology: For two semesters, I worked under a professor at CU Boulder in a research methods class to help students along the learning process - I held office hours and study sessions for exams.
  • Behavioral Counselor, The City of Boulder: The city has a program every summer for kids with various emotional and behavioral problems to attend a summer camp that is specifically structured to ensure success considering their difficulties. In my camp, I had kids with PTSD, schizophrenia, major depression, obsessive compulsive disorder, and more.

The truth is, all of us struggle at one point or another. Whether it is during the death of a loved one, a rough breakup, or just a period of depression, we all could use the help of a trained professional to jump back up to our feet a bit quicker.
