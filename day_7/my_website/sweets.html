Grilled Peaches with Whipped Cream

Warm peaches with whipped cream! Perfect for warm and cold weather, you can eat these delicious little treats on a summer evenings or cold nights, and no matter what they're a crowd pleaser. Original recipe at Bon Appetit.

  • Firm but ripe peaches
  • Sugar
  • vanilla
  • 1/2 cup chilled whipping cream
  1. Prepare barbecue (medium-high heat). Arrange peach halves, cut side up, on baking sheet; sprinkle peaches with sugar. Let stand until sugar begins to soften, about 10 minutes. Place peaches on grill, sugared side down. Grill until tender and slightly charred, about 4 minutes per side. Transfer peach halves to plates.
  2. Using electric mixer, beat whipping cream and vanilla in bowl until peaks form; Spoon whipped cream alongside peaches and serve.

Coconut Macaroons

I'm really not a big sweets person. One year Mimi asked me what type of birthday cake I wanted, without thinking I said mashed potato with brown gravy icing. It's really what I wanted and it's what I got, and it's still the best birthday cake I've ever had. My wife, however, has quite the sweet tooth-- she even saves pie for breakfast. So we had to find some sweets that we could both get down on. We both love these Macaroons from allrecipes.

  • 2/3 GF flour
  • 1 bag sweetened flaked coconut
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  1. Preheat oven to 350Â°. Line baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Stir together flour, coconut, and salt in a large bowl. Add milk and vanilla, stir until well blended.
  3. Drop dough in balls onto prepared baking sheet with small icecream scoop, spacing about an inch apart. Flatten slightly.
  4. Bake for 12 to 15 min, until edges begin to brown.
