Chia Seed Pudding

I'm a big fan of chia seeds for one simple reason, not that they are a great source for fatty acids, antioxidants, fiber, iron, and calcium. No no, it's that I like to squish them through my teeth into my cheecks. Jello was a favorite dessert of mine and as a kid I'd take a big bite, clench my jaw and push the cubes through the cracks in my teeth. Surely you all remember doing that too right.....right? Well, when I eat chia I'm back to pushing jello through my teeth only without the red dye 40, gelatin, and crazy amounts of sugar. Not to mention this is one of my wife's favorites, these little guys can be made the night before and are super easy. She found it flipping through one of our many allrecipes magazines.

  • 1 cup unsweetened vanilla-flavored almond milk
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • fruit (your choice)
  • maple syrup
  • nuts (your choice)
  1. Whisk almond milk, yogurt, 2 tablespoons maple syrup, vanilla, and salt together in a bowl until just blended; add chia seeds, whisk to incorporate, and let the chia seeds soak for 30 minutes.
  2. Stir the chia seed mixture to redistribute seeds that have settled throughout the mixture. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate 8 hours to overnight.
  3. Add fruit and nuts to the top and drizzle with maple syrup

Gluten Free Scones

I've flirted with eating GF for many years, I'd be pretty good about it for a while and then one day a savory scone would get the better of me. I'd tell myself "you'll just have this little bit that fell off the edge". But telling myself these things was a slippery slope, as by the end of the week I'd be cutting an entire scone in half and making a mac and cheese sandwich out of it. My latest attempt at going GF has been more succseful; I know my triggers so I knew I needed to find a decent scone recipe. Note - I don't live in some make believe world were I think someday I'll find a gluten substitute that will be as good as the real thing, I know I won't. But when I want something that's pretty darn close I make these from epicurious.

  • 1 3/4 cups Gluten-Free Multi-Purpose Flour (I use Namaste)
  • scant 1/3 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon xanthan gum (if not included in flour mix)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) frozen butter
  • 3/4 cup additions
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup cold milk
  • 1 teaspoon gluten-free vanilla extract
  1. The day before put stick of butter in the freezer, after a few hours take it out and grate it, put it back in the freezer over night.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, xanthan gum and salt.
  4. Work in the butter until the mixture is crumbly with small bits of butter remaining.
  5. Gently mix in additions
  6. Whisk together the eggs, milk, and vanilla until frothy
  7. Add to the dry ingredients, stirring until well blended
  8. The dough should be cohesive and very sticky. Place on a gf floured surface. Pat out to about 1 in. thick
  9. Use a icecream scoop and place on prepared pan.
  10. Brush with milk and sprinkle with sugar (optional)
  11. Put back in freezer for 15 min
  12. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes or until golden brown.
  13. Remove from oven and let sit for 5 minutes

Breakfast Egg Fried Rice

One of the great joys of ordering delivery Chinese food is the amount of food that will arrive at your door in 30 to 45 min. I'm always amazed at the amount of food I can pack away when I eat Chinese. There's a nasty rumor out there that says it's because of the MSG which stops telling your brain when you're full and should stop eating. That or I simply cannot control myself when I comes to that wonderful umami flavor explosion. Either way it's a problem, and I often feel so full I quickly slip into food comma and can't really remember the rest of the night. One very rare morning we had a carton of left over rice. Craving fried rice I did a quick search for breakfast Chinese because, apparently I didn't get enough the night before. I found this gem at My Korean Kitchen.

  • 2 eggs
  • Cooking oil
  • 1 cup day old cooked rice
  • 1 tbsp Tamari (GF soy sauce)
  • 1/2 tbsp seasame oil
  • Bacon or suasage
  1. Beat the eggs in a mixing bowl.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium high heat. Once heated, add some cooking oil. Reduce the heat to medium. Pour in the beaten eggs. When the egg starts cooking, break the egg into smaller pieces using a fork or chopsticks.
  3. Add the rice when the egg is partly (70 – 80%) cooked and stir the rice evenly. Add the soy sauce and gently mix with the rest of ingredients. (Optional) Add some sesame oil, sesame seeds and green onions then gently toss to coat.
  4. Serve
