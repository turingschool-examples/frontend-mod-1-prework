Competitive Gaming

A Master of the Fist

by Carlyle Douglas

Ever since I was a young child, playing Shaq Fu on the Sega Genesis (did you know Shaq had a fighting game??) with my older cousin in Brooklyn, in the genre of video games, fighting games has been a personal favorite. The idea that two people could compete against one another in a contest of skill and mental fortitude appealed to me far more than the typical platformers seen on the console at the time.

This love of fighting games would continue to manifest itself well into my teenage years, graduating from casual competition with friends to competing in official tournaments, and eventually earning a sponsorship to travel and compete in gaming events all across the state of Florida. While I no longer seek to make a career of it, the love for the competition of fighting games has never faded and has allowed me to compete at the highest levels in the largest tournaments in North America.
