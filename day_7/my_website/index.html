Winter Weather Travel Destinations

When the temperatures drop and the snow starts to fall, not all of us dream to escape to a white sandy beach. Let's take advantage of what the cold weather can offer us while traveling in the winter.


Vail, Colorado

There are many ski resorts to choose from in Colorado. They all offer something unique. Some cater to the rich and famous, some to locals. Some have great skiing, some great ammenities. Vail is my personal favorite. The skiing is phenomenal. Skip the front side of the mountain and head straight back to the bowls. That's where the magic happens!

  • Where to stay: The Sonnenalp
  • What to do: Ski!
  • Where to eat: Sweet Basil

Park City, Utah

When traveling to Park City there is something to offer for every outdoor enthusiast. Of course you can hit the slopes skiing, but you can also go to the olympic village, shop through their towns and find many family friendly activities. On my most recent trip I went tubing at one of their tubing parks and it was a blast. They allow you to go just once or go for an entire day. One time was enough for me, but it was thrilling!

  • Where to stay: Stein Eriksen Lodge
  • What to do: Go Tubing!
  • Where to eat: Riverhorse on Main

Germany

I traveled to Germany as a high schooler, and saw did the regular tourist things. I saw old churches, ate bavarian pretzels and I even tried to sneak a beer! Most memorable of my time there was a trip to the world's largest cuckoo clock. However, what I have been dying to do is visit the Christmas Markets. Use the information below to visit your Christmas winter wonderland!

  • Where to stay: Mandarin Oriental Munich
  • What to do: Go to the Christmas Markets!
  • Where to eat: Although there are many Christmas Markets, the one in Munich offers the greatest variety of food.

Iceland

Iceland is not as cold as it sounds, yet continues to provide a once in a lifetime expreience! It is easiest to center yourself in Reykjavik and venture out from there for activites. There are lots of things to do, but if you can catch the northern lights you are truly lucky! Another must is a stop to the blue lagoon. It is located in a lava field near Grindavik and is a show stopper!

  • Where to stay: Black Pearl Reykjavik
  • What to do: Go to the Blue Lagoon!
  • Where to eat: Kol Restaurant

Canada

Whistler

Whistler is a world class land of winter fun! Located less than two hours outside of Vancouver, it is an ideal place for locals and tourists to flock to. It has a lovely ski village, winter ziplining, and snowshoeing to name just a few activites, but make sure to stop by the olympic village. The olympics were hosted in Vancouver/Whistler in 2010 and the rings are still standing. Make sure to stop by for a photo!

  • Where to stay: Nita Lake Lodge
  • What to do: Go to the olympic Village!
  • Where to eat: Il Caminetto

Banff

Banff is located in Alberta, Canada and is quite picturesque. There is even a gondola that takes you high above Lake Louise for a better view of the beauty that surrounds you. Banff also offers an icewalk, cave tours and hot springs. Sign me up immediately!

  • Where to stay: Fairmont Banff Springs
  • What to do: Go to Lake Louise!
  • Where to eat: Coyotes Southwesten Grill
