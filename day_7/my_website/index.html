Toyota Engines

Some info on a few of my favorite Toyota platforms

We'll touch on the following:

  • 2JZ
  • 2ZZ
  • 3RZ
  • 22RE

    • 2JZ

    Perhaps the most legendary item in this article. The 2JZ is a 3.0 litre inline-6 cylinder engine. It came in a variety of models, some with forced induction (turbo), others with variable valve timing (VVT-i). All models featured solid/closed-deck cast-iron blocks, with aluminum cylinder-heads.

    2ZZ

    The 2ZZ, often referred to as Toyota's "Flagship 4-cylinder". The 2ZZ is a 1.8 litre inline-4 cylinder engine. Utilizing the same aluminum block from the 1ZZ, the 2ZZ featured an aluminum cylinder-head designed and manufactured by Yamaha. This cylinder head featured "lift" technology, coined by Toyota as VVTL-i. (Variable Valve Timing with Lift). The Yamaha head boasts a redline of over 8,000 rpm with the "Lift" kicking in around 6,000 rpm. The valves will open a little longer and wider for extra flow, both from the intake and exhaust manifolds.

    3RZ

    The 3RZ is another inline 4 cylinder, this time with a cast-iron block and aluminum cylinder head. The 3RZ is the beefier version of the 2RZ, boasting a massive 2.7 litres of displacement. One of the largest displacements of any 4-cylinder engine on the market.

    22RE

    The 22RE is the last inline 4 cylinder on my list. Nothing super special other than incredible reliabilty. Most older Toyota pickups were equipped with this 2.4 litre workhorse. This engine, like many others, also featured a cast-iron block and aluminum cylinder-head.
