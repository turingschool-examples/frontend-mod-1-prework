Sushi: More Than Raw Fish

A Very Brief History

The earliest examples of sushi can be found as early as the 2nd Century in South East Asia near the Mekong River Delta, where farmers would ferment fish by using rice and vinegar in order to preserve the fish for non-immediate consumption. Initially consumers would eat just the fish. However in Japan around the 9th century, rice and other forms of flavor would be added and the fish would not get to the point of fermentation, creating what we commonly think of as sushi. Over time, different types of fish and preparation methods would be developed in order to create one of the most elaborate and diverse forms of food.

Different Types of Sushi
