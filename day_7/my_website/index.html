Learning Tarot for Fun, Not Fortune

I have been interested in tarot cards for a long time. I'm not a big believer in the mystical or fortune-telling aspect of the cards. However, I love the illustrations: each card is packed full of stories and symbolism. Plus, its a really great party trick! Just for fun, I took a class on tarot reading this summer. I'm still very much a novice reader but here are some things I picked up!

The Rider-Waite Deck

While not the first tarot deck, the Rider-Waite deck is the most popular one in existence. It was created in 1910 and illustrated by Pamela Colman Smith. Most beginners start with this deck because the illustrations on the cards are very traditional and generally easier to read than more modern decks.

The Deck

A tarot deck consists of 78 cards divided into two groups:
The Major Arcana (22 cards) and the Minor Arcana (56 cards).

The Minor Arcana

The Minor Arcana, not so coincidentally, look a lot like our modern day playing cards. The only real difference between the regular deck and the tarot deck is the addition of the Page card. In each suit, the Page falls after the 10 and before the Knight (what we call the Jack). Instead of Clubs, Diamonds, Hearts and Spades, the four suits of tarot are:

  1. Swords
  2. Pentacles
  3. Cups
  4. Wands

The Major Arcana

The Major Arcana cards generally hold more weight than the Minor Arcana cards in a reading. In other words, if you are dealt a Major Arcana you should pay attention to it! The Major Arcana are (in order):

  • The Fool
  • The Magician
  • The High Priestess
  • The Empress
  • The Emperor
  • The Hierophant
  • The Lovers
  • The Chariot
  • Justice
  • The Hermit
  • Wheel of Fortune
  • Strength
  • The Hanged Man
  • Death
  • Temperance
  • The Devil
  • The Tower
  • The Star
  • The Moon
  • The Sun
  • Judgement
  • The World
