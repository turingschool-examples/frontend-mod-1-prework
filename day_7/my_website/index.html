A Pastry Chef Is Creative.

When becoming a pastry chef you must be creative. Creativity is hard to come by. You need to be open to different flavors, designs, and changes in the food industry. You need to welcome basil and red bean flavor, abstract designs (much like splattering coulis), and gluten free, vegan, and dairy free options. Creativity is a must.

  • Creativity In The Kitchen

A Pastry Chef Is Adaptable.

Adaptability is something a pastry chef must have. You must be able to work fast and be concise with your plating, sizes of muffins, and texture of your macarons. If a diner comes in and want's a dairy free dessert, well that means you need to give them one, but you will already have something up your sleeve because you adapted already. This is just a pastry chef.

  • Adaptation: The Elegant Ascent of Emma Bengtsson

A Pastry Chef Is Hard Working.

Hard working is what every pastry chef is. You need to wake up early and stay late, but this doesn't phase you. You love the rush. You bake 100 vanilla cakes everyday, make 3 fillings from scratch, and beat 2 pounds of Italian butter cream during the weekdays. Don't even get you started on what the weekends look like. All you know is that you are a pastry chef, hard working is just what you are.
